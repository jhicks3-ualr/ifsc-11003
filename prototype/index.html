Okay, so there I am in Brodeaux, France, at my first café and for the life of my I have no idea what I'm looking at. The menu was overwhelming, and when I finally just took a blind leap and said "Affogato", out of sheer curiousity, I was met with confused looks and stares. Come to find out I had just ordered a dessert drink for breakfast. So after spending a week there trying everything on the menu, getting a French to English dictionary, and some trial an error I figured, I'd share my knowledge with the world. So welcome to my "Guide Pratique" or my Practical Guide to coffee. Below is a list of every beverage I tried, it's ingredients and my personal taste rating.

Notes and Comments

My favorite beverage by far was the Cappucino, delicious and perfect for breakfast. The latte is basically a Cappucino, just in half. The Affogato was delicious, and for a big wake up I recommend the Café au Lait, gets you alittle shaky, but at least you'll be alert.

Home Café

Now I'm sure you're wondering, "Can I make any of this at home?" and as they say You miss 100% of the shots you don't take! -Wayne Gretzky --Michael Scott. So below will be a few lists of what you need for your own home café!

Hardware needed

  1. Espresso Machine, preferrably 1 with a milk steamer
  2. A handheld milk frother wand doesn't hurt either
  3. A Coffee Grinder
  4. Espresso Tamper with scale
  5. A stainless steel milk frother measuring cup

What you'll drink

  • I recommend Oatly if you use Oatmilk, it has the best consistency of the oatmilks.
  • Coffee is subjective, but for espresso I recommend anything dark roast, and Fidel & Co. has a delicious dark roast called Origin
  • And of course, ice cream, chocolate syrup, and cream for any sweet drinks
